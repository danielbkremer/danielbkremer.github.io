General acclaim

Endorsements across films & work

“Daniel Kremer does not lack for ambition, persistence, bravery, and talent, and his work deserves a wider audience.”

Michael Fox, KQED/NPR

“Daniel Kremer is for real. He makes films right now and out of thin air. Other people try to raise money for a film and complain. Daniel raises money, makes two films, writes a book, and gives thanks. He's unstoppable...and on his way up!”

Rob Nilsson, Sundance Grand Prize winner and Cannes Caméra d'Or winner

“A brave, dedicated, fearlessly creative new voice in American cinema.”

Henry Jaglom, indie film maverick

“Daniel Kremer is the rabbi of noir.”

Philip Kaufman, director of The Right Stuff, The Wanderers, Invasion of the Body Snatchers

“Daniel Kremer is a natural and skillful filmmaker. His films are enormously impressive and imaginative.”

Gerald Peary, celebrated film scholar and veteran critic for The Boston Globe

“Daniel Kremer definitely has the tools and directorial firepower to speak to a broader audience. He displays a remarkable command of the film medium, of all the ways filmmakers convey meaning and emotion to the audience: lighting, composition, staging, sound, editing, and music used to powerful effect. This command is very rare to see, and it's all much harder than Kremer makes it look. There is poetry in the beauty of his filmmaking.”

Eugene Corr, director of Desert Bloom and Waldo Salt: A Screenwriter's Journey

“Daniel Kremer is surely a directing talent to watch. Many sequences feature evidence of a budding master at work.”

F.X. Feeney, film critic (L.A. Weekly, Z Channel Magazine, People, Vanity Fair)

“His filmmaking is so confident that it's astonishing. The perfectly calibrated camera moves, the always surprisingly but ineffably right compositions, and the precision of the cutting remind me of Jacques Rivette and Paul Thomas Anderson.”

Michael Glover Smith (director of Mercury in Retrograde and Relative), White City Cinema

“Kremer has a long history of creating thought-provoking, immensely involving films on budgets that leave you gasping for air wondering 'How can he possibly do that?'”

Richard Propes, The Independent Critic

Acclaim for Cruel, Usual, Necessary: The Passion of Silvio Narizzano

Selected excerpts

“Gripping, spectacularly done, utterly convincing. I am humbled by the lyricism of Kremer's narration.”

Patrick McGilligan, Author of Alfred Hitchcock: A Life in Darkness and Light and George Cukor: A Double Life

“An overwhelming documentary, with an impressive and exhilarating sense of commitment. It does a remarkable job of shedding serious, responsible light on a neglected director, through the eyes of a literate, observant, talented filmmaker who appreciates the things most others miss.”

Nat Segaloff, Author and Film Historian, Author of Hurricane Billy: The Stormy Life of William Friedkin

“I've been plunged into an awareness of an unknown director's work numerous times in books, but I daresay this is the first I've gotten this from a documentary. I have no doubt Silvio Narizzano would be humbled by this account of his life's work and grateful to have found an interpreter with Kremer's sensitivity, empathy, and an unwillingness to flinch comparable to his own.”

Tim Lucas, Video Watchdog

“Quite extraordinary. Kremer has a gift for digging up directors who have fallen between the cracks, and his insightful video essays and documentaries leave me feeling like I have a lot of catching up to do with films and filmmakers I managed to miss. Keep an eye out for Cruel, Usual, Necessary: The Passion of Silvio Narizzano.”

Alonso Duralde, The Wrap, MSNBC, Author of Hollywood Pride (TCM)

“One of the best films of 2024.”

Bill Ackerman, The Auteurist Trap

Acclaim for Overwhelm the Sky

Selected excerpts

“An endlessly intriguing, breezily enigmatic yarn that hangs out in the unincorporated territory between waking and dreaming, reality and paranoia.”

Michael Fox, KQED/NPR

“The movie is quite a trip! It's a tantalizing puzzle; words cannot begin to describe the experience of seeing it. The black-and-white cinematography is stunning.”

Filmmaker Magazine

“The work of a young virtuoso.”

Peter Medak, director of The Ruling Class, The Changeling, and The Krays

“One of the year's best motion pictures. Ambitious, thoughtful, and beautifully constructed, filmed in pristine, visually arresting black-and-white. Alexander Hero gives a remarkable performance. Though it runs nearly three hours, you simply won't find yourself looking at your watch.”

Richard Propes, The Independent Critic

★★★★ “An ambitious epic of vast scope that is a real testament to what filmmakers can achieve with only a microbudget. The film is full of twists and turns, and earns its lengthy runtime. Aaron Hollander’s striking black and white cinematography is particularly effective.”

Hannah Sayer, U.K. Film Review

“Daniel Kremer has mounted quite the ambitious and challenging film, on a minuscule budget. The black-and-white cinematography by Aaron Hollander is gorgeous and adds a classical noir atmosphere to the film, and the editing [is] seamless. Thanks to a great screenplay, impressive direction, and naturalistic performances, there is a lot to enjoy about this subdued mystery.”

Bobby LePire, Film Threat

“The filmmaking is quite meticulous, shot with the care of a perfectionist. The shots demand to be felt. Overwhelm the Sky is a neo-noir, but Daniel Kremer tinges it with something of his own, a quality that is in all of his films.”

Theo Alexander, Unsung Films

“Highly recommended! The film runs nearly three hours, however Kremer doesn’t waste an inch of his runtime. Every single story element has a purpose, reminding me very much of Christopher Nolan’s Memento. Alexander Hero's performance is electric and the technical mastery on display is simply astounding.”

Ben Cahlamer, The Movie Revue

“The film showcases a filmmaker who isn’t afraid to push audiences all the while keeping a steady hand to guide them through a long, strange trip. Aside from Kremer’s keen direction, another impressive part of Overwhelm the Sky is the acting, particularly from Alexander Hero.”

Don R. Lewis, Hammer to Nail

(Critic's Pick) “Dress up a little (just like people did for the roadshows of the ’60s), get your program, and settle in for a unique afternoon of movie entertainment, complete with good music and conversation. Daniel Kremer certainly has a story to tell.”

G. Allen Johnson, The San Francisco Chronicle

“A model of artistic ambition on a low budget.”

Dennis Harvey, 48 Hills
